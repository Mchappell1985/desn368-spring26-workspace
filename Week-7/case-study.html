Case Study: Tomodachi Life Guide

Design Decisions

So many of my design decisions were based off of the fun and playful look of Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream's Mii creator menu. The bright yellows and oranges were color picked specifically from a screenshot of the game, and the background was recreated in figma to match the one within the game. Galindo was my choice of font due to it being the closest I could find on google fonts that matches.

Technical Challenges

The sidebar positioning by far has been and still continues to be an issue for me. As of submitting this assignment my sidebar is still technically "broken" because it won't stick to the top of the webpage one you scroll down far enough in a desktop viewport. So it's stuck on the side of the page in the middle.

Responsive Strategy

Switching to a hamburger menu for mobile viewports, and utilizing the techniques I learned with the earlier flexbox assignment really helped the way I was planning to tackle my responsive design. I still continue to troubleshoot with sticky, fixed, and absolute properties when it comes to the sidebar in the desktop viewport, but I enjoyed working witht he hamburger menu and scroll properties in the mobile viewport.

Learning Moments

My biggest learning moment had to be that your properties applied to the sidebar can MESS UP your formatting if you stack to many properties in one container. As I have stated previously I am still wrestling with the sidebar in desktop viewport.

Future Improvements

Future improvements would 100% include fixing the sidebar issues in desktop mode. Though besides that, I would like to improve the placement of my images on the side, make them bigger without interfering with my body text and headers. Also if there was ever a way to play music on the webpage, I think that would be silly.
