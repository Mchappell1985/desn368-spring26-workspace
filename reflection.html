Design Reflection

A short reflection on what I learned throughout the quarter. This page is for my DESN 368 Course.

The Threshold

What was the specific moment this quarter where code clicked for you?

There was not necessarily a single moment where coding itself "clicked" for me because I already had experience with programming before taking this course. Having worked with other languages, I found HTML much easier to understand, since it is a markup language and its syntax is significantly more straightforward than many traditional programming languages. My threshold moment happened after I finished the Figma layout for my Field Guide website. For the first time since starting this course, I was excited to begin coding a project because I already had a clear visual direction, and I could see how the design would translate into a functioning website. That experience helped me view coding less as a technical obstacle or a chore (as I have viewed it as such from previous experiences) and more as another tool for bringing my designs to fruition.

The Pit

What was the hardest assignment? How did you get yourself out of the pit of despair?

The most challenging assignment for me was the Field Guide project. While I felt comfortable building the overall structure of the site, responsive design and viewport resizing created several issues that I heavily disliked dealing with. It was frustrating to create a layout that looked good on one screen size only to discover that it was completely broken when viewed on another device. I worked through these issues by experimenting with media queries, testing different layouts, and spending time troubleshooting each problem individually rather than trying to solve everything at once. Although the process was difficult, the feeling of completing the project was mentally rewarding. Looking back, the challenges I faced during that project are the reason I feel significantly more confident in my ability to build responsive websites.

The Pivot

How does your final portfolio compare to your original Moodz and Vibes?

My final portfolio is drastically different from the original Moodz and Vibes concept I created at the beginning of the quarter. The largest reason for this shift was that my Visual Communication Design final project focused on developing my personal brand. During that process, I created a personal logo, wordmark, color palette, and overall visual identity that felt much more representative of who I am as a designer. Once that project was complete, it made sense to incorporate those branding elements into my portfolio rather than continuing with my original direction. As a result, my portfolio became more cohesive because it directly reflects the visual identity I developed for myself with that final project.

The Process

What would you change about how you approached this course?

If I could change one thing about how I approached this course, it would be my time management, particularly at the beginning of the quarter. I work full time while also attending classes, and I underestimated the amount of time required to complete some of the assignments. Had I known beforehand how much work would be involved, I would have started projects earlier and planned my schedule more carefully. There were several instances where I found myself balancing multiple deadlines at once, which made the process way more stressful than it needed to be. Pacing large projects and giving myself enough time to troubleshoot unexpected issues is important. I plan to be better prepared in future courses.

The Future

How do you see this hybrid skillset impacting your future practice?

I believe this hybrid skillset of design and code will be extremely valuable in my career. Understanding both sides of the process allows me to think more critically about how a design will function once it is implemented. Even during this quarter, some of the projects I created generated interest from potential clients, which has landed me internship work over this summer [2026]. Moving forward, I see coding becoming another creative tool in my workflow alongside illustration, branding, and visual communication design.
