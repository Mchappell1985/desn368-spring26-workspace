Shigeru Miyamoto

The Godfather of Videogames

Shigeru Miyamoto’s work has shaped the entire video game industry. His creations have sold millions of copies worldwide and influenced generations of designers.

Early Life

Shigeru Miyamoto grew up in rural Kyoto, where he spent much of his childhood exploring forests, caves, and lakes. These early adventures later inspired the sense of discovery found in his games.

He developed an interest in art, music, and storytelling from a young age. Miyamoto attended the Kanazawa Municipal College of Industrial Arts, where he studied industrial design. Though not initially focused on video games, his creative instincts would soon find the perfect outlet.

Major Works and COntributions

  • Super Mario Bros. (1985)
    Revolutionized platform gaming and introduced side-scrolling mechanics.
